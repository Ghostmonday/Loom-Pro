Privacy Policy

Neural Draft LLC · Effective date TBD · Last updated June 15, 2026

Neural Draft LLC ("Neural Draft," "we," "us," or "our") operates the Gaijinn software platform, including its command-line interface (CLI), web interfaces, APIs, and related services (collectively, the "Service"). This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information when you access or use the Service.

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our practices, do not use the Service.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to information we process in connection with your Neural Draft account, your use of the Gaijinn CLI and APIs, communications you send to us, and data generated when the Service analyzes your repositories or executes sprints.

This policy is intended primarily for users located in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the United States, you understand that your information may be processed in the United States and other jurisdictions where we or our service providers operate.

2. Information We Collect

Account and Contact Information

When you register for or use the Service, we may collect your email address, account identifiers, name or organization name (if provided), authentication credentials, billing contact details, and support communications. Payment card data is processed by our payment processor and is not stored in full by us.

Repository and Project Data

To provide architectural analysis, blueprint generation, and sprint orchestration, the Service may process:

  • Repository snapshots and structural metadata, including file paths, dependency graphs, import relationships, and derived metrics;
  • Configuration files relevant to project analysis;
  • Prompts, intents, and blueprint inputs you supply; and
  • Outputs such as gravity/curvature metrics, work-unit decompositions, and sprint artifacts (for example, under .gaijinn/).

The core CLI workflow is designed to operate locally where possible. Cloud-hosted or API-connected features may transmit project data to our infrastructure for the duration necessary to perform the requested operation.

Sprint and Operational Logs

When you run atomic sprints or grid orchestration, we may collect sprint timestamps, worker assignments, execution status, integrity scores, complexity indices, billing-relevant usage metrics, and diagnostic telemetry necessary to operate and secure the Service.

Technical and Usage Information

We automatically collect IP address, browser type, device identifiers, CLI version, API request metadata, log files, and cookies or similar technologies on our websites where applicable.

3. How We Use Information

We use collected information to provide and improve the Service, authenticate users, generate analyses and blueprints, calculate credit consumption and integrity-based pricing, provide support, detect fraud and abuse, and comply with legal obligations.

We do not use your repository content to train public or third-party foundation models unless we obtain your separate, explicit consent for that specific purpose.

4. What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell your personal information. Neural Draft does not sell, rent, or trade PII to data brokers or unrelated third parties for their independent marketing purposes.
  • We do not disclose trade secrets or proprietary algorithms except as necessary to provide the Service, as required by law, or with your direction.
  • We do not publicly publish your private repository contents or sprint logs except where you explicitly configure sharing or export features.

5. Trade Secret Protection

Neural Draft treats certain Gaijinn platform elements as confidential trade secrets, including proprietary weighting models, gravity engines, decomposition kernels, and related know-how. We implement administrative, technical, and contractual safeguards to restrict access, limit subprocessors to need-to-know obligations, and respond to unauthorized disclosure in accordance with applicable law.

6. Sharing and Disclosure

We may share information with service providers, professional advisors, affiliates, law enforcement when required by law, and successor entities in connection with a merger or acquisition. We do not share PII with third parties for their own independent advertising purposes.

7. Data Retention and Security

We retain information as long as reasonably necessary to provide the Service, meet billing and legal requirements, and resolve disputes. We employ industry-standard safeguards including access controls and encryption in transit. You are responsible for safeguarding your credentials, license keys, and local project artifacts.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your U.S. state of residence, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or obtain a portable copy of certain personal information. To exercise these rights, contact privacy@neuraldraft.net. We may verify your request before responding.

9. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to individuals under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Contact privacy@neuraldraft.net if you believe we have collected information from a child.

10. Governing Law

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of the United States and the state in which you reside, without regard to conflict-of-law principles, except where preempted by federal law. Disputes may also be subject to the dispute resolution provisions in our Terms of Service.
